What Is a Mozilla Campus Club?

Mozilla’s mission is to ensure the Internet is
a global public resource, open and accessible to all. A Mozilla University & College Club is a group of students with a passion for technology who meet regularly to advance this mission by building and innovating on open source projects that keep the web open.

Mozilla Firefox VIT-Bhopal

Mozilla Firefox VIT-Bhopal is an interactive club. We know that web development has a high scope in the future. We also know that our college has many talented students. Most students don't get appropriate guidance and opportunities to outcast their skills and advance their career in the field of web development. Our club will help individuals to get their correct domain so that they can work precisely in their field and grab the best placement. Our club will work as the voice of students.

The History Of The Club

Long before Mozilla’s first formal student program, “Campus Reps”, opened its doors in 2008, students have been playing a crucial role in the development of Mozilla and the web itself.

The student-built Lynx browser was one of the first and Mosaic (Firefox’s distant ancestor!), pioneered by students and staff, opened the graphical web to millions. Many of the original community members who worked on the first firefox in 2002 were students just like you.
